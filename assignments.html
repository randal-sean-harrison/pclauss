Thursday (8/23)
Argument Is
  • For Tuesday's class, print and read these excerpts from Aristotle's On Rhetoric. Begin bringing this document to every class. Also, while reading, consider: Although On Rhetoric was written well over 2,000 years ago, where do you notice that Aristotle's points ring true even today? What stands out to you as noteworthy, interesting, challenging, or puzzling?
  • Also for Tuesday, examine this document, showing the structure of a sample "micro-argument." After either printing the document or copying the structure in your own notes, try to ascertain the purpose of each of the six phrases or clauses: What is the function of each category in the structure? What is each category's relationship to its counterparts?
  • If you have a laptop or tablet that's easy to carry, bring it to class on Thursday, August 30. You'll also need electronic access to Writing Exercises #1 and #2. (#2 has not yet been assigned.)
